Hum Aligner

The hum aligner is a Python script I made that aligns hum audio to its equivalent speech audio. The purpose of this is to generate audio samples with the highest difficulty of information masking.

The algorithm works by extracting the non-silent intervals of the hum audio and aligning them to the word(s) they correspond to; many words can be mapped to the same hum interval. The hum interval is then resized to match the length of the word(s).

Tongue Estimator

The tongue estimator is a Flutter application that uses audio recorded from your phone to estimate the shape of your tongue using the resonances produced. It makes use of a formant extractor that was written from scratch. I learned about how linear predictive coding worked and then coded it to use in my formant extractor.

The drawing of the tongue was not the priority of this project, so forgive its simplistic look.

Glottal Stop Coarticulation Research

I did a research on the coarticulation dampening properties of the glottal stop. I used acoustic data to show that although some people say that the glottal stop doesn't influence coarticulation, it is actually able to lessen the effects of coarticulation in certain conditions.
